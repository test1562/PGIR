PGIR: Enhancing Control and Expressiveness for Zero Shot Instruct TTS via Perception-Guided Instruction Refinement

Abstract

In the field of controllable speech synthesis, Instruct TTS has recently emerged as a key technology for a wide range of applications, including virtual assistants and audiobook narration, allowing users to control speech style through natural language instructions. However, Instruct TTS still faces significant challenges in achieving precise acoustic control and expressiveness, primarily due to ambiguous user instructions during inference and instruct texts in training datasets that do not correspond to human-perceived speech attributes.To address these challenges, we propose: (1) a Perception‑Guided Instruction Refinement (PGIR) Mapper that disambiguates and clarifies user instructions, (2) a dual‑path Instruct TTS framework that jointly leverages the original and refined instructions, and (3) a human‑perception‑calibrated instruct annotation pipeline for training data. Experiments show that our approach significantly improves controllability and expressiveness in zero‑shot settings. Audio samples are available at https://t1235-ch.github.io/pgpt/.

Comparison Experiments

This work introduces two core innovations: a new Instruct TTS paradigm integrating the PGIR module, and a perception-guided speech annotation pipeline. To validate the effectiveness of these innovations, two sets of comparative experiments are conducted:

  • Zero-shot Instruct TTS:
    • Compare with the open-source Zero-shot Instruct TTS model CosyVoice2
    • Compare with CosyVoice2 (with PGIR proposed in this paper) to verify PGIR's generalization ability
    • Further contrast CosyVoice2 (with PGIR) with our model (Dual-path Instruct TTS with PGIR) to validate the effectiveness of Dual-path Instruct TTS, focusing on instruction following and expressiveness in zero-shot scenarios
  • Data Pipeline Comparison:
    • With the same Llama-based TTS architecture, contrast models trained via SpeechCraft (generic instruction pipeline)
    • Compare with models trained via our Perception-Aware Speech Annotation Pipeline to evaluate the impact of annotation quality

Ablation Study

To verify the necessity of key components in our framework, we conduct two ablation experiments by removing critical modules from the proposed model.

  • -w/o PGIR: This variant removes the Perception-Guided Instruction Refinement module entirely. Results show a drop in IF Score and increased ambiguity in interpreting abstract user instructions, confirming PGIR’s irreplaceable role in refining control signals.
  • -w/o Perception: This variant omits the human listening test calibration step in the annotation pipeline, leading to a decrease in IF Score. This is because speech attributes often mismatch human perceptual judgment, highlighting the importance of aligning annotations with human auditory experiences.
